Today's Exercises

tap to expand · rest arms every other day

arms — 5 lb dumbbells
Stand feet shoulder-width, dumbbells with underhand grip, elbows bent 90° so forearms are parallel to floor near waist. Brace core, then push dumbbells forward and slightly upward to shoulder height. Contract front shoulders and biceps at the top, keep shoulders down and back. Slowly reverse to start. No momentum — controlled push only!
Stand feet shoulder-width, hold dumbbells at chest level, elbows slightly bent. Slowly rotate both arms in a wide circular path up and around. Keep core tight, glutes engaged, torso upright. Go clockwise for a set then counterclockwise. Smooth and controlled the whole way through!
Hold dumbbells at thighs, palms down. Raise both arms straight forward to shoulder height. Lower slowly. Keep a slight bend in the elbows throughout — don't lock out.
Stand feet shoulder-width, dumbbells at sides, palms forward. Curl up toward shoulders keeping elbows pinned to sides. Lower slowly — that's where the work happens. Don't swing your back!
Dumbbells at shoulder height, elbows 90°, palms forward. Press straight overhead until arms nearly straight. Core tight, don't arch lower back. Lower with control.
Hold one dumbbell with both hands overhead, arms extended. Keeping upper arms close to your head, bend elbows to lower the weight behind your head. Squeeze triceps to press back up. Don't flare elbows out — keep them pointing forward the whole time!
core — no equipment
Forearms and toes, body straight head to heels. Hips don't sag or pike. Breathe. Start at 20-30 sec if needed and build up. This is the one daily non-negotiable — do it every single day!
On back, knees bent, fingertips behind ears (not pulling neck). Curl shoulders up, exhale at top, lower slowly. Abs only — no momentum.
Flat on back, legs straight, hands under hips. Raise legs to 90°, lower slowly and stop just before touching the floor. That hovering tension is the whole point — great for lower abs!
